Dog Health & Wellness Guide

Preventive care isn't just about avoiding vet bills — it's about giving your dog more healthy years. Here's what the science says, and what you can do about it today.

80%+ of dogs over age 3 have some form of dental disease Source: AVMA
56% of dogs in the U.S. are overweight or obese — linked to joint disease, diabetes, and shortened lifespan Source: APOP / Banfield
1 in 4 dogs will develop cancer in their lifetime; early detection dramatically improves outcomes Source: AKC / Veterinary Cancer Society
2-3 years preventive care can extend a dog's life, according to AAHA guidelines Source: AAHA

These numbers aren't scare tactics — they're a roadmap. Small, consistent preventive care habits compound into years of extra tail wags. Here are the five areas that matter most.

🐛

Deworming & Parasite Prevention

Intestinal worms, heartworm, fleas & ticks

Parasites are more than a nuisance — they can cause anemia, malnutrition, organ damage, and in the case of heartworm, death. The Companion Animal Parasite Council (CAPC) estimates that 1 in 100 dogs in high-risk regions tests positive for heartworm annually. Intestinal parasites like roundworms, hookworms, and whipworms are even more common, often showing no symptoms until the infestation is severe.

  • Heartworm prevention — monthly oral or 6-12 month injectable; year-round recommended by AHS
  • Broad-spectrum dewormers — covers roundworms, hookworms, whipworms, and tapeworms
  • Flea & tick control — topical, oral, or collar; critical for Lyme disease and tapeworm prevention
  • Fecal testing — at least annually, more often for puppies or high-risk dogs

Key fact: Many monthly heartworm preventatives also cover common intestinal parasites. One pill can protect against 3-5 types of worms — making compliance dramatically easier.

Oral & Dental Care

Toothbrushing, dental chews, water additives, professional cleaning

Periodontal disease is the most common clinical condition in adult dogs, affecting over 80% of dogs by age 3 (AVMA). The bacteria from dental disease don't stay in the mouth — they enter the bloodstream and can damage the heart, liver, and kidneys. The good news: daily brushing and annual dental checkups can nearly eliminate this risk.

  • Enzymatic toothpaste — dog-specific (never human toothpaste — xylitol is toxic)
  • Dental chews & treats — VOHC-approved options mechanically reduce plaque
  • Water additives — easy daily supplement that reduces bacteria in the mouth
  • Professional dental cleaning — under anesthesia, recommended every 1-2 years by AAHA
  • Dental wipes & sprays — alternatives for dogs that won't tolerate brushing

Key fact: Brushing just 3 times per week makes a measurable difference. Daily brushing is ideal, but even intermittent brushing reduces periodontal disease risk by 60-70% compared to never brushing.

Ear Care & Cleaning

Ear cleaners, infection prevention, ear mite treatment

Ear infections are one of the top reasons dogs visit the vet — especially for floppy-eared breeds like Cocker Spaniels, Basset Hounds, and Labradoodles. The warm, moist environment inside a dog's ear canal is a perfect breeding ground for bacteria and yeast. Regular cleaning with a vet-approved solution can prevent most infections before they start.

  • Ear cleaning solutions — drying agents + antimicrobial ingredients; use weekly for at-risk breeds
  • Ear wipes — convenient for between-clean maintenance and on-the-go use
  • Ear mite treatments — especially important for puppies and multi-pet households
  • Post-swim/bath drying — moisture is the #1 trigger for ear infections

Key fact: Dogs with floppy ears are 3-5x more likely to develop ear infections than dogs with upright ears. Weekly cleaning with a drying solution can reduce infection rates by up to 80% in at-risk breeds.

Skin & Coat Care

Shampoos, conditioners, hot spot treatments, allergy relief

Skin allergies are the #1 reason dogs visit the veterinarian (AKC). Environmental allergens, food sensitivities, and contact irritants can cause itching, hot spots, hair loss, and secondary infections. A dog's skin is also a mirror of their overall health — dull coat, excessive shedding, or chronic itching often signal an underlying issue. Consistent grooming and targeted skin care products can dramatically improve quality of life.

  • Medicated shampoos — antifungal, antibacterial, or soothing (oatmeal/aloe) depending on condition
  • Hot spot treatments — sprays, creams, and hydrocortisone for acute flare-ups
  • Omega-3 supplements — fish oil reduces inflammation and improves coat quality from the inside out
  • Hypoallergenic grooming products — fragrance-free, dye-free for sensitive dogs
  • Paw balms & nose butters — protect paw pads from heat, ice, and rough surfaces

Key fact: Atopic dermatitis (environmental allergies) affects 10-15% of all dogs. Regular bathing with a gentle, moisturizing shampoo can wash allergens off the skin and reduce itching by 50% or more without medication.

Nutritional Supplements

Joint support, probiotics, multivitamins, calming aids

Supplements fill the gaps that even a great diet can leave — especially for aging dogs, active dogs, and dogs with specific health conditions. The right supplements, backed by veterinary research, can improve mobility, digestion, immunity, and quality of life. Not all supplements are created equal: look for products with NASC (National Animal Supplement Council) certification and published clinical research.

  • Joint support — glucosamine, chondroitin, MSM, green-lipped mussel; critical for seniors and large breeds
  • Probiotics & digestive enzymes — restore gut health after antibiotics or stress; improve nutrient absorption
  • Omega-3 fatty acids — EPA/DHA from fish oil for brain, joint, skin, and heart health
  • Multivitamins — fill micronutrient gaps in homemade or limited-ingredient diets
  • Calming aids — L-theanine, CBD, chamomile, melatonin for anxiety, fireworks, and separation stress
  • Milk thistle & liver support — especially important for dogs on long-term medications

Key fact: A 2021 clinical study found that dogs receiving glucosamine + chondroitin supplementation showed measurable improvement in mobility within 6-8 weeks, with effects comparable to NSAIDs in mild-to-moderate arthritis — without the gastrointestinal side effects.
