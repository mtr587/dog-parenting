Dog Health & Supplement Guide

The right supplements can keep your dog moving, playing, and feeling their best — at every age. Here's what the science says, and what's worth your money.

Why supplements matter

• Arthritis is common — many dogs develop arthritis as they age, and joint supplements like glucosamine are one of the most popular OTC remedies recommended by vets. They're safe for long-term use and work best when started early.

• Consistency beats dosage — most supplements take 6–8 weeks of daily use to show visible results. Skipping days or switching products too often is the #1 reason they don't work.

Nutritional Supplements

Joint support, probiotics, multivitamins, calming aids

Supplements fill the gaps that even a great diet can leave — especially for aging dogs, active dogs, and dogs with specific health conditions. The right supplements can improve mobility, digestion, immunity, and quality of life. Not all supplements are created equal: look for products with NASC (National Animal Supplement Council) certification — it means the product has passed quality and safety audits.

  • Joint support — glucosamine and chondroitin help maintain cartilage and reduce inflammation; especially important for seniors and large breeds
  • Probiotics — good bacteria that support digestion and immunity, especially helpful after a course of antibiotics or during stressful periods
  • Omega-3 fish oil — rich in healthy fats that improve skin, coat, joint, and heart health
  • Multivitamins — fill nutritional gaps in homemade or limited-ingredient diets
  • Calming aids — natural options like chamomile, L-theanine, or melatonin for anxious dogs, fireworks, and separation stress

Key fact: Glucosamine and chondroitin aren't just for senior dogs. Starting them early, before signs of arthritis appear, can slow joint wear and tear — and many vets recommend them as a long-term investment in your dog's mobility.
